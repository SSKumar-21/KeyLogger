Understanding Keyloggers

Keyloggers are tools or software designed to record the keystrokes made by a user on their keyboard. They can be used for both legitimate purposes, such as monitoring productivity or recovering lost data, and malicious activities, such as stealing sensitive information.

How Do Keyloggers Work?

Keyloggers can work in several ways:

  • Software-based: Installed on the system to capture keystrokes.
  • Hardware-based: Physical devices attached to keyboards or computers.
  • Browser-based: Scripts that record input fields on websites.

Types of Keyloggers

Keyloggers can be broadly classified into the following types:

  • Hardware Keyloggers: These are physical devices connected to the keyboard or placed within the hardware. They are difficult to detect and typically require physical access to install and retrieve.
  • Software Keyloggers: These are programs installed on a device to monitor and record keystrokes. They can be standalone applications or hidden within legitimate software.
  • Kernel-based Keyloggers: These operate at the kernel level, making them powerful and difficult to detect. They intercept keystrokes directly from the operating system.
  • API-based Keyloggers: These use standard APIs (Application Programming Interfaces) provided by the operating system to capture keystrokes. They are easier to detect compared to kernel-based keyloggers.
  • Remote Keyloggers: These can be used to monitor devices over a network, allowing attackers to access keystrokes without physical access.
